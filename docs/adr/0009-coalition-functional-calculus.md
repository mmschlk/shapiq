# Coalition Functional Calculus

Every cardinal interaction index and generalized value is a linear functional of the game whose coefficient on ``v(K)`` depends only on ``|K & S|`` and ``|K|`` — expanding the discrete derivative of SII, the anchor of the transforms, or the gain/loss pair of a bloc marginal all land in the same factored form. This decision makes that representation an explicit derived object, the coalition functional: a per-interaction-size coefficient table computed mechanically from the weights an index already declares through its capability. The derivation plays the role differentiation rules play in autograd — declare an index through its weight formalism (including one defined by a user via ``define_cardinal_index`` or ``define_generalized_value``) and the machinery downstream of the formalism comes for free, instead of each estimator family carrying hand-written per-index implementations.

Two consumers exist. The exact explainer contracts the table densely over the powerset, which collapsed the previously separate cardinal and generalized-value branches into one; the existing exact test suite passing unchanged is the evidence that the derivation reproduces every shipped index. The ``MonteCarlo`` approximator importance-samples the same table: its ``CoalitionSizeSampler`` draws coalition sizes proportional to the functional's total absolute coefficient mass (uniform within a size), and every sampled coalition enters the estimate with its coefficient divided by its sampling probability, while the empty and grand coalition are seed samples contributing their coefficients exactly. This yields an unbiased anytime estimator for *any* index with a derivable functional — the SHAP-IQ construction, generalized from a per-index derivation to a capability-level one. Sizes with zero mass are never sampled, so degenerate kernels (an order-capped Moebius transform touches only coalitions inside its interactions) get support-restricted samplers without anyone writing them. The mass-proportional proposal is deliberately generic rather than variance-optimal: the permutation walks and the kernel-matched regression estimator remain as bespoke, lower-variance methods for their closed index sets, exactly as ADR 0007 argued; what changed is that "not derivable from any declared capability" no longer describes sampled estimation as a whole.

Aggregation became a third capability rather than a dedicated solver: an ``AggregationIndex`` declares a base index and superset-aggregation coefficients depending only on the size difference, and both the exact explainer and ``MonteCarlo`` estimate the base and aggregate linearly — which preserves exactness and unbiasedness by construction. k-SII now declares itself as the Bernoulli aggregation of SII, replacing its dedicated exact path; this refines ADR 0008, which kept the solver dedicated because a capability wide enough for k-SII, FBII, and kADD-SHAP would have described "some least squares problem". The aggregation capability is narrow — it selects a linear superset sum, not a regression — so it selects behavior without that dilution. FBII and kADD-SHAP keep their dedicated regression solvers: argmin-defined indices are a genuinely different primitive whose sampled estimation runs through the kernel-matched regression family, not through a coalition functional.

Opening the index-name registry falls out of the same decision: with indices definable through a formalism, the closed name literal and validation set became unrepresentable, so ``InteractionIndexName`` widened to ``str`` with non-emptiness the only global rule. Shipped names stay documented, and name collisions remain the definer's responsibility — explainers were already forbidden from dispatching on names, so nothing behavioral can hang on them. The risk accepted here is aesthetic (arbitrary strings on explanations), not semantic.
